About Jhulkhet

Jhulkhet is a hidden gem nestled in the hills of Western Rukum, located in Musikot Municipality Ward 6. It is made up of three smaller villages: Tangtara, Jhinjaa, and Tyakhola. With 50 households and over 200 residents, Jhulkhet offers a peaceful rural lifestyle. Despite being in a remote area, the village is well-connected to nearby towns and cities, making it easier for people to travel and access services. Surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, Jhulkhet is a serene place to live.

  • 5 km northeast of the Mid-Hill Highway
  • 5 km from the Rapti Highway
  • 12 km from Salley Airport
  • 8 km from Musikot Khalanga, the district headquarters

Jhulkhet has also become a key business hub for previous VDC like Sankh, Chaukhabang, and Bhalakcha.

Life and People of Jhulkhet

Jhulkhet is a close-knit community where most people are from the Chhetri,Bahun and Magar ethnic groups. Life here mainly revolves around farming, with locals growing rice, maize, and vegetables. Many families also raise livestock like cows, goats, and chickens. Small shops along the village paths sell basic goods such as flour and tea. The younger generation often dreams of getting government jobs, while some look for work opportunities abroad. In the evenings, families gather around wood fires, sharing time together. Life in Jhulkhet is simple and deeply connected to farming and family.

Education & Infrastructure

Education
Jhulkhet provides education to children from nearby villages through two important schools: बालकल्याण मा.वि. झुल्खेत, मुसिकोट-६, रुकुम, पश्चिम and Shree Rukumeli Bright Future English Medium Boarding School. These schools play a vital role in offering both traditional and English-medium education, helping to uplift the community's academic standards.

Infrastructure
Jhulkhet is developing rapidly, especially with the construction of the Sitalpokhari-Jhulkhet-Chunbang Highway, which has boosted connectivity. The village is emerging as a commercial hub, with several local factories contributing to the economy. The reliable electricity supply from the Sankh Khola Jala Vidhyut hydropower project further supports this growth.

Health Sector
In terms of healthcare, Musikot Municipality Lab and private medical clinics provide essential health services to the residents. These improvements reflect the village’s commitment to enhancing both infrastructure and the quality of life for its people.

Sport Sector
Jhulkhet is home to Tyakhola Rangasala, a popular venue where sports activities like cricket, volleyball, and football are held. It serves as a key center for local sports events. In recent years, the मुसिकोट नगरपालिका राष्ट्रपति रनिङ शिल्ड तथा नगर प्रमुख कप was organized here, showcasing the community's enthusiasm for sports. Additionally, the annual टयाखोला गोल्ड कप takes place during the Dashain festival, further highlighting the village's active participation in sporting events.

Connectivity & Transportation

Jhulkhet is connected by gravel roads and lies close to important hubs like Musikot Khalanga (8 km away) and Salley Airport (12 km away).

  • It is also near key highways such as the Mid-Hill Highway and Rapti Highway, both 5 km away.
  • The village is located on the Sitalpokhari-Jhulkhet-Chunbang Sadak and serves as a junction point for multiple roads, including Jhulkhet-Tin Bahini Sadak, Jhulkhet-Chaukhabang Sadak, Jhulkhet-Aaduwabari-Hil Sadak, and Jhulkhet-Bhalakhcha-Ratapahar Sadak.
These connections have made Jhulkhet an important business hub and strategic location in the region.

Cultural Heritage of Jhulkhet

Cultural Heritage
Jhulkhet is rich in cultural traditions, with one of its most celebrated expressions being the Lok Pauranik Paiseru Naach. This traditional dance is performed by the पौराणिक लोक नृत्य सास्कृतिक समूह (Pauranik Lok Nritya Sanskritik Samuha), a local cultural group dedicated to preserving the village’s folk traditions.
Visitors are often captivated by these vibrant dance performances, which showcase Jhulkhet’s deep cultural history.
The village celebrates major Hindu festivals like Dashain, Tihar, and Chhath with great enthusiasm, while traditional dances like सिङगारु and मयुर नाच are integral parts of these festivities. Known for its rich cultural heritage and hospitality, Jhulkhet welcomes new people with open arms, making it a truly unique place to visit.
